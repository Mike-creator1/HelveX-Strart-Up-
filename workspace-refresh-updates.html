Overview

AI setups go stale faster
than they're built.

The first version of an AI workspace is the one with the most attention. Six months in, prompts have drifted from the way the team actually writes, agents are running on tools that have already shipped two new versions, and nobody can remember which integration owner approved what.

Workspace Refresh Updates is the standing answer to that. Every cycle a HelveX engineer treats your workspace as if they were rebuilding it today — what would they keep, what would they replace, what hasn't earned its keep — then ships the change set under your sign-off.

It's the work that almost never gets prioritised internally, and the reason most AI deployments quietly underperform a year in.

What's included

Six workstreams. Every cycle.

A predictable scope of work, delivered on the same cadence each month, written into the SLA.

01

Prompt re-tuning

We pull the last cycle's logs, find where outputs drifted, and rewrite the prompts that need it — version-controlled, diffable, reviewable.

02

Agent ship cycle

One or two new agents shipped per cycle from your open backlog — scoped, built, tested in your workspace, handed over with a runbook.

03

Integration refresh

Every connector — CRM, email, calendar, ticketing, knowledge base — kept on its supported version. Deprecations handled before they break the workspace.

04

Cost & performance audit

Token spend by agent, latency by route, error rates over time. We rebalance toward Ether for high-volume work and Prometheus for the calls where depth pays.

05

Model routing review

Workloads re-mapped across Ether, Nexus, and Prometheus based on the previous cycle's outcomes — not based on what was guessed at setup.

06

Executive brief

A short written report at the end of every cycle — what changed, why, what it cost, and the next cycle's priorities. The version that survives the meeting.

Business benefits

The compounding case
for not letting the work decay.

Lower cost

Routing reviews and prompt cleanups typically reduce token spend cycle-over-cycle, even as usage grows.

Higher quality

Outputs stay aligned with how the team actually writes, decides, and approves — instead of drifting against an old launch spec.

No silent breakage

Connector deprecations, API changes, and policy updates are caught and fixed before they show up as "the AI suddenly stopped working."

Audit trail

Every change is logged with reason, owner, and date — the documentation your security and compliance teams actually want.

Process

One cycle.
Repeated every month.

The same five steps every cycle. Predictable, scoped, written into the SLA — so there's never a surprise about what you're paying for or when it lands.

01

Pull & analyse

Cycle kickoff & log review

Pull the previous cycle's interaction logs, error reports, cost breakdown, and any tickets raised by your team. Build the change list from real signal.

Day 1–3
02

Scope & sign-off

Change list, prioritised & approved

A written change list goes to your workspace owner with effort, risk, and expected impact per item. You decide what ships this cycle — nothing happens without sign-off.

Day 4–6
03

Build

Implement the approved change set

Prompts re-tuned, agents shipped, integrations refreshed, model routing updated. All work happens in a staging copy of the workspace before anything hits production.

Day 7–22
04

Verify & promote

Side-by-side validation, then ship

New version runs in shadow against production traffic. If outputs are equivalent or better and cost is in target, the change set is promoted to live. If not, it goes back to step 03.

Day 23–27
05

Brief

Executive write-up & next-cycle priorities

A short written brief: what changed, why, the cost delta, the open backlog, the next cycle's likely priorities. The version that survives the meeting.
